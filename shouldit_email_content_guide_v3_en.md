## TONE RULES

**Specific over generic**
- Always use real numbers: "$124", "44 other blenders", "9.1/10", "$89"
- Always use real product names: "NutriBullet Full-Size", "Vitamix 5200"
- Never use: "this product", "our top pick", "a great deal"

**Editorial verdict, not a data dump**
- Don't just show numbers — give a judgment
- Be clear: "Worth it / Not worth it"
- "We tell you if it's worth buying now or not" — not "here is the data"

**Specific trust lines**
- Never: "We respect your privacy"
- Always specific: "We only email when there's a genuine price drop. Not every sale."
- Always specific: "One email with our verdict. Not a newsletter unless you ask."

**Low pressure — always limit the commitment**
- "One email. No spam."
- "Not a newsletter unless you want it."
- "Max one email per month."

**Short, action-specific CTAs**
- "Alert me →" / "See full scores →" / "Find my blender →"
- Never use: "Subscribe", "Sign up", "Get started", "Learn more"

**"We" voice — team/lab, not a bot**
- "We tested", "We track", "We tell you", "We reply"
- Never: "Our system will notify you", "You will receive"

**Email openings — never use "Hey"**
- Go straight to the verdict or data
- First sentence must contain a specific number or score

## PART 4 — WEEKLY NEWSLETTER
> Sent manually via Resend Broadcasts
> Only to subscribers with tag engagement:active (opened ≥2 emails)

**Subject line formula:**
```
[We tested X] + [Specific finding] + [Implicit reader value]
```

**Good examples:**
```
✅ "We tested 12 air fryers. One model won by a lot."
✅ "The Prime Day deal everyone's sharing — we tested it. Here's the truth."
✅ "Should you wait for Black Friday? Our honest answer for 6 products."
✅ "This $60 blender beat a $300 one. Here's why that's unusual."
```

**Bad examples:**
```
❌ "Our weekly newsletter" — too generic
❌ "Best deals this week!" — sounds like spam
❌ "You won't believe what we found" — clickbait
```

**4-section template:**

**THE VERDICT** (~150 words)
One product. Test result. Clear recommendation — no hedging.
Use specific scores. Use specific prices.

**WORTH BUYING RIGHT NOW** (~100 words)
1–2 deals from tested products only.
Always include context: "This is its lowest price in 6 months."
Never just "Great deal!" — must have a basis.

**SKIP THIS** (~100 words)
One product getting hype that isn't worth it.
Specific reason from test data — not a general opinion.
This is the section people forward to friends.

**QUICK TAKES** (~50 words, 3 bullets)
Fast. Specific. No links needed.
- "Instant Pot Duo is on sale again. Still our top pick. Still worth it."
- "Finishing coffee maker tests next Friday — 18 models."
- "Avoid the viral knife sharpener on TikTok. Tested it. Works twice then stops."
